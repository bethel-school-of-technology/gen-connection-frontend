Welcome to Generation Baby Boomers



Generation Baby Boomers is a term used to describe those born between 1946 and 1664. This group represents almost 20% of the American public. As the largest generational group in U.S. history (until the Millennials showed up), baby boomers have had and continue to have a significant impact on the culture. Baby boomers emerged after the end of World War II, when birth rates across the world spiked. The explosion of new infants became known as the baby boom. During this 18 year span, approximately 77 million babies were born in the U.S. alone, comprising nearly 40% of the American Population. The sheer size of this generation magnified its impact on society. As the boomers reached young adulthood in the 1960's and 70's, their tastes in music and their hair and dress styles strongly influenced the national culture, and the political activism of some contributed to the unpopularity of the Vietnam War.
